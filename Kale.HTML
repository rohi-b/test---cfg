Kale

Kale is one of the most nutrient-dense foods on the planet, it has vast health benefits as it is loaded with powerful antioxidants such as quercetin and kaempferol. It is also one of the best sources of vitamin K and C. A portion of kale contains around 33 calories, 6 grams of carbs and over 3 grams of protein, it contain little fat. The fat that it does contain is an omega-3 fatty acid called alpa linolenic-acid.

    Nutritional Benefits

  • Kale is well known as a superfood due to it's nutritional density.
  • It contain Vitamin B such as folate, this provides energy to the body and helps to produce healthy blood cells.
  • Kale also contains Vitamin C and E, both which support a healthy immune system. However kale also contains Vitamin K which helps to heal wounds.
  • As for minerals, kales contains most of the everyday minerals we need including potassium, calcuim, magnesium and selenium.

    Health Benefits

  • Kale is a good source of beta-carotene, the body uses this is convert into Vitamin A, this is necessary for the growth and maintenance of body issue, including for skin and hair.
  • Kale has benefits for the prevention of diabetes, heart diseases and cancer.

    Fun Fact

  • Kale is found in many health products, even in nail varnish!
